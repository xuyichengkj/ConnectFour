Introduction

Connect Four (also known as Captain's Mistress, Four Up, Plot Four, Find Four, Fourplay[citation needed], Four in a Row, Four in a Line and Gravitrips (in Soviet Union)) is a two-player connection game in which the players first choose a color and then take turns dropping colored discs from the top into a seven-column, six-row vertically suspended grid.

The game was first sold under the Connect Four trademark by Milton Bradley in February 1974.

Rules

Object: Connect four of your checkers vertically, horizontally or diagonally in a row while preventing your opponent from doing the same.

  • The game grid is 6 rows x 7 columns
  • Choose one color for your discs
  • Take turn dropping discs from top of one column
  • The pieces fall straight down, occupying the next available space within the column
